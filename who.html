Hi! I’m Vincent

I’m currently studying to become a DevOps Engineer at Chas Academy. I was born on 8 November 1991—the year the Soviet Union dissolved. At the cinema you could catch Terminator 2, The Silence of the Lambs, and Home Alone, and one of my favorite tracks, “Lily Was Here” by Dave Stewart, was on the radio.

I grew up in Täby, just outside Stockholm, with my parents and two younger brothers. My first real contact with IT came in my early teens when I took a two-week course in Dreamweaver and built a simple website. I didn’t continue with web development back then, but I did (and still do) build my own desktop PCs.

How I Got Into Coding

In my mid-twenties I dabbled with Arduino projects, but fast forward to late 2024: while studying mathematics to apply for an economics program, I found a bonus section about Python in the digital course material. I started experimenting there and got hooked on programming.

I didn’t pass the math exam—but I didn’t let that stop me because I had just found a new passion: coding. I began self-studying Python and soon built my first functional program: a simple travel-time calculator based on speed and distance. Since then I’ve created several programs in Python, C, and C#. My true strength isn’t one specific language—it’s my drive and focus. When I’m into something, I deep-dive and learn fast. That mindset now fuels my path into DevOps. I want to be able to master a lot of programming languages!
